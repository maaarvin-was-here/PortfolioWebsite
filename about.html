Marvin Lin

Engineer // Software Developer

I am a second-year at the University of Washington pursuing a double degree in engineering (with a focus on aerospace) and computer science. I love synthesizing robotics and code to design solutions for problems around me. Outside of work, I enjoy astronomy and astrophotography, playing chess, and doing crosswords.

Currently...

I am working at the University of Washington's Applied Physics Laboratory as a Software and Robotics Engineering Intern. My project combines the desiging and building of structures and devices with the writing and deployment of code to make them run smoothly and efficiently.

I am also involved in research at the University of Washington's Transformative Robotics Laboratory, where I am prototyping a perfectly planar mechanism, a device that is geometrically constrained to move in only two dimensions.

Aside from my laboratory commitments, I am working on (too) many side projects, some of which are a Magic Mirror™, a TensorFlow powered chess engine, and a security system with computer vision.
