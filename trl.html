As a research intern and NASA Space Grant scholar at the University of Washington's Transformative Robotics Laboratory, I designed, built, and tested prototypes for a novel robotic joint mechanism. I created a number of CAD designs (drawings and models) using SolidWorks. I also utilized rapid prototyping methods to quickly make and test different iterations of various components.

I especially enjoyed working with the lab's Carbon M1 3D Digital Light Processing (DLP) printer, for which I learned printing, handling, and curing methods for a variety of different resin materials including UMA90 and RPU70. Thanks to a combination of accurate CAD modeling and the Carbon's extreme precision, the parts came out of the printer fairly well. However, I would always perform more precision machining using machine shop tools and small hand tools in order to account for small variances and get the part to be sized just right, contributing to more effective and valid testing.
